Mental Health Prototype
Mind Mooder Prototype
An emotional wellness tracking app designed to help users reflect on and regulate daily moods. The prototype was developed through a human-centered process — starting with empathy mapping, followed by low-fidelity wireframes, usability testing, and iteration. The final design prioritizes low-friction daily check-ins, mood pattern visualization, and gentle nudges toward healthier emotional habits.

Health UX HCED 450
Pill Pro
A medication management concept developed as part of HCED 450. The goal was to reduce medication errors through intuitive interface design, smart reminders, and clear visual hierarchy. User research revealed that elderly and chronically ill patients struggled most with complex pill schedules — so the solution centered on a simplified daily view, confirmation flows, and caregiver sharing features. The project involved journey mapping, competitive analysis, wireframing, and a high-fidelity Figma prototype.

About me

I'm Zack — a Human-Centered Engineering Design student at the University of Michigan–Dearborn with a passion for designing products that feel right to the people who use them.

I grew up in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam, moved to Michigan during high school, and volunteered with Loaves & Fishes in Taylor before starting university. Those experiences shaped how I see design — not as aesthetics, but as care made tangible.

My process is rooted in empathy, curiosity, and honest iteration. I care about the real person sitting on the other side of every interface, product, or system I work on.
